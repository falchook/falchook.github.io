ABOUT BEING SELFIE-MADE I hope you embrace the phrase. Being selfie-made does not mean that you are selfish or self-absorbed. It just means that you are a person who loves designing your own life and living by your own terms. Being selfie-made means you are creative, an out-of-the-box thinker and like the idea of being your own boss. SELFIE-MADE MILLIONAIRE I consider myself selfie-made. I also live to help others be the best they can be and am willing to sacrifice my time and energy to help others transform their lives for the better. Still, I am selfie-made and therefore love to live an inspired life by building life and business authentically and with a lot of fun. I’m from Rockford, Illinois and moved to New York City, opting out of college to pursue my dream of working in the Media/Entertainment business at the age of 18. I owned a successful live event production company in New York City before falling in love with social media. I guess I’ve always been naturally entrepreneurial and therefore psychologically unemployable, preferring to be self-employed rather than being confined to a corporate office (although there is nothing wrong with that). My production company became an elite go-to company for celebrities and socialites in the New York City area. We enjoyed rubbing elbows with the rich and famous and playing music at the Rainbow Room, Plaza and Waldorf Astoria. Eventually, we wanted something even more exciting. AND we saw an opportunity in Internet marketing and social media. SELFIE-EMPLOYED In 2008, the economy and entertainment industry were PONZIED. We were hit hard financially and made a decision not to rebuild in a field that we were losing interest in. So we said goodbye to live event production and music and took a dive into an industry that had huge potential. I had a natural knack for creating communities online and entertaining people with my selfies. I applied this ‘knack’ to my passion for helping others get fit and fabulous with a stellar nutrition and super foods company that sold its products via a Network Marketing model. WHO KNEW? Apparently my poses, filters, beautifying apps for my ‘selfe’ passion would turn me into a SELFIE-MADE MILLIONAIRE in 3 years. It may have been beginners luck or maybe the fact that people want to be entertained on social media . It worked to my benefit that I was an entertainment professional (with a smart phone) who made people laugh and enjoy the journey of ordering products that made them feel gorgeous. I don’t know. But I do know that I worked hard and discovered that there is massive opportunity on the Internet. AND IT IS FUN BEING SELFIE-MADE AND SELFIE-EMPLOYED. FAITH AND FAMILY Oh, yeah, did I mention that I have a huge family? I also wanted to be selfie-employed because I am the mom of 7 children (8 if you count my son-in-law and 9 if you count my dog Ladybug). I have adopted 5 children (from Ethiopia, Guatemala, Ukraine) and have birthed 2. We live near Disney because I want my kids to believe that dreams really do come true. We built a huge team within our direct sales business of families who have adopted, are adoption advocates and love helping others get healthy. Even though we embrace the term SELFIE-MADE we know that we have been blessed through prayer and our intense faith in a power higher than us. SELFIE-EDUCATED I am all for striving for excellence and expertise, but I’ve observed that many people wait to take the necessary steps to become a top leader until they perceive themselves to know everything. Build your business as you are learning. Be a great student but be willing to also commit. Too many people buy trainings, invest in opportunities but then they fail to push their way into their success. You have the ability to crush this business. But it takes work and it is really up to you and your believe. BELIEVE IN YOUR SELFIE At the end of the day you have to believe in your Selfie. Your mindset rules everything. I had to battle doubt and lack of confidence to build my self-confidence so that I could work hard to achieve my goals. Einstein said, “Nothing happens until something moves.” Our success relies on us taking action to learn, to grow to intentionally battle doubt. LOVE THEY SELFIE (and THY NEIGHBOR) I hope you learn from my coaching, insight, experience and friendship. We are all still learning but I’m excited to share what I know. At the end of the day, love thy selfie, but also have faith knowing that there is a CREATOR greater than you that has built you perfectly in His image…. the sky is the limit because you are beautifully and wonderfully made. Deanna
